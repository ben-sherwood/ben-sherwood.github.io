Learning Plan

My Strengths:

Communication is something I would say I do very well. I have grown up in a very open and honest family which has helped me be forthcoming with my problems. In the last 4 years I have been medicated for MDD (Massive Depressive Disorder) and this has further helped my communication skills. I am willing to ask for help when needed and admit when I am struggling. Working for many years in hospitality has helped my communication by giving me experience working with complete strangers and colleagues alike – very different to family and friends and has encouraged my communication style to become adaptable.

Honesty is another trait that I think is vital to good communication as communication isn’t possible if you are not completely open with your peers. I do my best to present all information to help make calculated decisions with others.

Perfectionism could be both negative and positive, and I will come to the downsides in my weaknesses. I try hard to do everything at 100%. I put a lot of pressure on myself to produce the best results I can.

Weaknesses:

Oh boy there are many. Like I said above, far easier to criticise oneself than complement.

I am a very emotional person. I often let my emotions rule my decision making rather than having a balance of emotion and rationality guide me. I call this balance of rationality and emotions wise mind, and I try and balance myself when I am stressed and tired to prevent myself being overwhelmed by emotions which can be debilitating.

Back to perfectionism. I often find that if I can’t do something exactly the way I want, I give up. I’d rather not do it than be disappointed with my result, and this has been a major player in the way I have lived my life. I am slowly getting better at dealing with this and hope it won’t be too much of a problem when it comes to this course.

I also often withdraw into myself when things get tough. This is something I have been better at recently, and rather than withdrawing I will communicate with people about why I feel like running away from what ever problem I have. Looking for support when I feel like this is essential to my sticking power.

How I will manage myself:

With my main strengths and weaknesses outlined, lets have a look at how I can manage my time and energy while completing this course.

Firstly, while my communication is a strength, I often don’t really communicate with myself, and this often leads to a snowball effect where I then get frustrated and withdraw into myself. Something I have discovered even just since starting foundations is being organized cures this. When I say communicating with myself, I mean outlining and vocalising what it is I want, and how I want to achieve this. I started using sticky notes on windows to do this, by outlining weekly and daily things I want to achieve. I split larger tasks into smaller ones so I can continually feel a sense of accomplishment as I work. I think this has been incredibly helpful and I have since upgraded from sticky notes to an app called todoist.

This app allows me to have multiple projects each with multiple tasks. I can set dates and times for each and drag and drop between different projects and deadlines. This makes managing my workload simple and also gives me the satisfaction of marking things as complete. If I need to do something for someone, or get help from someone, I add these in as tasks. This again makes it easy for me to remain true to my commitments and keep up those channels of communication.

Seeking help isn’t something I should be afraid of, we learn by failing and reflecting, and often part of that reflection comes in the form of advice from others. My plan for seeking help is this:

Encounter problem – I look at the problem, try and break it down, and give myself a time limit to solve the problem on my own. If I still am struggling with a problem, I will seek help from my peers on slack. While waiting for a response I will keep on troubleshooting. Hopefully by this stage I will have solved the issue on my own, if not the advice from others should be another tool to solve the issue. If all else fails after these steps my final place to turn is my facilitators. This is a last resort as I feel the best way to learn is to teach yourself, and asking others and helping others is also a great way to help yourself, and them learn. I’m assuming my facilitators are gods, their time is better spent changing the world, not fixing my nav bar issues.

What I do want to see from my gods is support. Encouragement, communication, advice and respect.

Values and Strenghts

My Values:

So interestingly I have done a lot of work around beliefs and values as I was doing a type of therapy called CBT. CBT is very value and goals focused. As part of this therapy I chose 6 values I feel are important to me. I got to 6 after going through a list of around 50. It was quite a challenge to do this!

  1. Self-Knowledge
  2. Contribution
  3. Growth
  4. Generosity
  5. Humilty
  6. Family

Self-Knowledge

Self-knowledge is very important to me. I was diagnosed with MDD (massive depressive disorder) and the only way to have any chance of recovering is to have a strong sense of self and to understand how I respond to the world around me. The more I learn about myself, the better I can deal with any difficulty that arises.

Contribution

Contribution is something I really hold as a core value. We can only progress as a community/ society/species if we make lasting and meaningful contributions to those communities. I want my legacy to be something lasting I have done for my community. At the moment I am passionate about making change in mental health awareness, I am just yet to decide how to do this.

Growth

Growth is similar to self knowledge. Growth is a biproduct of self knowledge as the more we understand about ourselves, the more we grow as a person. Growth to me also means improving my understandings of the world, my skills as an individual and my connection with my surroundings.

Generosity

Generosity is exactly as it sounds. being generous with my time and my wealth. Offer it when and where I can.

Humilty

Humilty is to conduct myself without ego, never assume anything about anyone, let my actions reflect who I am. Be humble in my failures and in my successes.

Family

Family is the cornerstone of my life. I stand upon the shoulders of my family, they are my giants. I want to continue this value, and help build on this value to ensure those new members can experience the love, kindness, and safety that my family has given me.

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

I recently was walking home from the supermarket in Newtown. I witnessed two girls begin to fight. They were very aggressive towards each other, punching, kicking and hair-pulling. Many people were standing around some were avoiding the situation by crossing the street.

I made a decision to stop the fight by putting myself between them, and quickly someone else came to help pull them apart. While I didn't have a huge amount of time to think about why I was doing what I was doing, I feel that I acted with humility - I didn't judge or assume, I just knew that if I was in that position, I would want someone to help deescalate the situation. It was also a situation that allowed me to grow as a person. I am not a confident person, nor am I physcially imposing but I stood up for what I believe in and decided to stop two young people making a terrible mistake. I made sure both girls were alright, so I guess you could say I was also generous? It wasn't a nice situation but I hate the idea of people standing around filming or laughing. Thats not the person I am.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

It's hard to say what my culture really is. I come from a family who encourage and respect openness, honesty, love, and diveristy. We thrive on our differences and celebrate thinking for ourselves and doing what we believe is right within our societies. I am lucky to be able to speak freely with my sisters and parents. This has given me an appreciation for listening to others and being open to changes. I value family so highly as I am lucky to be from an incredibly caring network of family members. They encourage growth, and we always push eachother to discover new things about the world, our communities, and ourselves. This openness and caring environment breeds humility and I am so greatful for that.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

Some of my strenghts are also weaknesses. For one, I am a perfectionist. I strive to be the best I can be when it comes to submitting work. Sometimes this prevents me from actually even starting as I am also so afraid of failure, or not being satisfied with the work I have done --- I think my brain also sees this as a failure. A big strenght of mine is being able to listen and understand people. I pride myself on my ability to interact with others and feel this will be a real asset when it comes to finding work, but also in learning as I can understand others well, which will help in learning from others as well as helping others when they may need it.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

I think my social skills will be vital on this learning journey as like I said above it means I am able to listen and understand other peoples ideas and perspectives, even if they are different to my own. When it comes to learning, experiencing problems from multiple angles can be incredibly benefical as sometimes the best solution is one you wouldn't normally think of. Being surrounded by other people and being able to interact, listen, and understand is vital (in my opinion) to learning. As far as obstacles go, being able to listen and communicate effecitvely can make mole hills of mountains. It can turn a very complex and daunting challenge into something fun and exciting, as you get to experience the issue from multiple different angles.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

This might be a stretch, but its a recent example and one which I think outlines qualitites in myself, and the other person involved. I was on the Spirit of New Zealand for a week with a group of 26 other people all aged between 18 and 65(ish). The Spirit is a tall mast ship that has no powered form of winches to haul the sails up. Many aspects of the boat are very physical and require the team to be cohesive. There was a situation where I was up one of the masts with a young girl, we were preparing the sails to be hauled after riasing the anchor. Part of this process requires you to walk out along the yard (the wooden beams that run horizontal to the mast) and push a sheet off the yard. While out on the yards you are anywhere from 15m to 30m high, rolling from side to side while having to work with your hands. Its challenging. I loved being up in the rigging - its exciting, physically challenging, and offers a breath-taking view of the sea and landscape around you. Karleigha, the girl who was helping me did not share my enthusiasm for being up there, but wanted to experience life on the ship fully. She was out on the yard with me next to her, and was trying to push the sheet off the yard. She was having difficulty and wasn't enjoying being up that high. She asked if we could swap, however once out on the yard you cannot move past another person. I offered her encouragement and support and gave her some tips that I had found helpful while doing the same process on the upper square sails. Being positive and encouraging in the face of this challenge I think was essential to us managing to finish the work on the sails. Karleigha got the chance to prove to herself that she could do it, and I think me helping not only gave her confidence but also gave myself confidence too. I had struggled and also found it challenging, so being able to relate while also offering things I had found helpful was a great way of removing the tensions we both had, and being positive and friendly also encouraged her to do the work - A fantastic experience in learning and challenge for us both. In the future I think I would use the same positivity and energy that I used in that situation, however many of the challenges I will be facing I wont have experienced before, so learning how to keep this positive energy while not knowing the solution will be vital. Its easy to lose hope when you dont know how to solve a problem. I think I like this "positive/encouraging" approach is good because it encourages people to find solutions themselves, which is immensly rewarding. Karleigha was beaming after her achievement, and rightfully so. She did something she didn't think possible of herself, and I can't wait to have many of those moments in my learning journey with Dev Academy

A guide to Margins, Borders, and Padding

To understand Margins, borders, and padding, first we must understand how html elements are viewed by the browser. Pretty much every html element has an invisible box around the content. Circular Image? It will have a square box with the circles diameter as the width and height.

Padding

Now, With the above in mind lets take a look at padding. Padding is the space between the content of the element, and the invisible box around the content.

To set padding in CSS it is very simple. Select the element you want to change using css, and add a padding attribute like this:

p{
padding-top: 5px;
}

This will add a 5px space between the content and the invisible box the content sits in. We can add more padding by using other variations of the attribute, like padding-right or padding-bottom, but there is an easier way!
Enter, padding. You can use just "padding" to choose all 4 sides at the same time like this:
p{
padding:5px 10px 5px 10px;
}.
This adds 5px padding around the content on all sides.
If you are using equal padding for each side, you can just use:
p{
padding: 5px;
}
This will automatically apply the 5px to each side. In terms of knowing which number will impact which side of padding, its simple: Think clockwise starting at 12. Top, right, bottom, left.
So, in our first example the top and bottom padding is 5px and the left and right padding is 10px.
Looks like you are pros... lets now define that invisible box... Onwards to borders!

Borders

Adding a border does just that. It adds a border to one or more sides of our "invisible" box. I often like to put a border around an element even if i don't plan on leaving it there when I am working out how I want to position the item, as it makes it easier to align elements.
Borders have many different combinations of attriubtes, I will show you the basic premise for creating a border, and you can see more advanced options at w3school.

p{border: solid black 1px}

This is a very simple border. It is a solid line, the color is black and it is 1px wide. There are many styles of border like dotted, dashed, double, and more. The color attribute will take either a standard color, or a hexidecimal code, like #92B359 - the green color used on this post border.
The above style is actually condensing several different css commands:

p{
border-style: solid;
border-color: black;
border-width: 1px;
}

Just like padding we can apply different styles to different sides of our element:

border-width: 1px 5px 1px 5px;

This would give you a top and bottom border 1px wide, and left and right a width of 5px. The same thing can be done for color and style:

border-color: red black pink green;

border-style: dotted dashed solid double;

Crazy colors. Crazy styles.

Margins

Hey look! We are onto margins already and its only 3am!
Margins work a lot like padding, except instead of increasing space between content and our invisible box (or visible if you have a border!) it increases space between the whole element and other elements around it.
Lets look at the css for this:

p{
margin: 5px;
}
p{
margin 5px 2px 5px 2px;
}
p{
margin-top:5px;
margin-right: 2px;
margin-bottom: 5px;
margin-left: 2px;
}
You can see its almost identical to the padding! The first example sets a 5px width for each side. - The second example sets each margin width following our clock top, right, bottom, left. The fourth and final example individually sets each margin.

So thats all there is to it! Borders, Padding, and Margins are essential for ensuring your website looks good and is layed out how you want. Play around, see what works, see what doesn't, and most importantly of all, have fun!
